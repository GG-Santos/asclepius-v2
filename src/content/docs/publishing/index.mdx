## Content lanes

| Lane | Admin responsibility | Review before publish |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Blog | Write and edit posts. | Title, slug, cover, body, links, status, and public formatting. |
| Team | Manage staff and instructor profiles. | Name, role, credentials, photo, and display order. |
| Testimonials | Add or approve quotes. | Consent, attribution, batch context, and approved state. |
| Assets | Upload reusable media. | File type, rights, quality, and whether it belongs in public content. |

## Safe publishing workflow

<Steps>

<Step>

### Draft privately

Create or edit the item in the dashboard. Keep posts unpublished and
testimonials unapproved while content, rights, or attribution are still under
review.

</Step>

<Step>

### Preview the public surface

Open the public page or preview route. Check names, media fit, text wrapping,
links, and mobile layout.

</Step>

<Step>

### Publish or approve

Publish or approve only after the public result is accurate. For graduate
quotes, keep consent evidence outside the app if your operating process requires
it.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Public content boundaries

- Do not edit registry data from content pages.
- Do not imply a credential is valid unless the verification page says so.
- Use Assets for reusable media, not as an audit log.
- Keep 3D models, videos, and images tied to a real content use.

<DocNote title="The registry should stay factual" tone="info">
Public content can support the training center brand, but credential pages must
stay factual, verifiable, and free from promotional claims.
</DocNote>
