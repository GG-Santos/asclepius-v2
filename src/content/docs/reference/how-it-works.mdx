---
title: How verification works
description: Where the data comes from, and why a verification result can be trusted.
---

Verification is deliberately simple: one registry, one record per credential, one
public result.

## The flow

<Steps>

<Step>

### A record is created

Staff add an EMT as a student, then **promote** them to graduate on
certification. That publishes a single record — keyed by LCN — to the registry.

</Step>

<Step>

### Anyone looks it up

A verifier enters the LCN. The registry finds the one matching record and derives
its current status — Verified, Expired, or Legacy.

</Step>

<Step>

### The result reflects the registry

The page shows exactly what's on file: profile, proficiency record, license card,
and certificate — or a clear "no active credential" if nothing matches.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Why it can be trusted

- **Single source of record.** The public result is the same record staff
  maintain — there's no separate, stale copy.
- **Read-only and open.** Verification needs no account and can't be altered by
  the person being checked.
- **Live status.** Expiry and Legacy are derived at lookup time, so a result is
  current the moment you load it.

## What verification does not do

It confirms a credential exists in the WSL EMS registry and shows its status. It
doesn't vouch for anything outside that registry, and an expired or absent record
means the credential isn't currently active here — see
[Credential statuses](/docs/verify/statuses).
