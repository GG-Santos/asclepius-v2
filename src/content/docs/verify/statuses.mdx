---
title: Credential statuses
description: What Verified, Expired, and Legacy mean — and what they imply about validity.
---

Every credential carries a status. Here's what each means and what it does — and
doesn't — say about validity.

<StatusReferenceTable />

## Verified

The license is active in the registry and not past its expiration date. The full
record is shown: profile, proficiency scores, license card, and certificate.

## Expired

The license is past its valid-until date. The record is retained, but the
credential is no longer current — the result page shows the date it expired.

## Legacy

A Legacy record belongs to **batch 16 or earlier**. Those batches were graded
before automated scoring, so detailed proficiency scores were never digitized.
Everything else — profile, license card, certificate — is present and valid.

<Callout type="info" title="Legacy doesn't mean invalid">
Legacy describes how scores were recorded, not whether the license is valid. A
Legacy credential is as valid as any other; only the detailed score breakdown is
absent.
</Callout>
